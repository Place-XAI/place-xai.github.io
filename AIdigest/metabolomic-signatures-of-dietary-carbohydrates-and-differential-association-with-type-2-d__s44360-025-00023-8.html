Metabolomic signatures of dietary carbohydrates and differential association with type 2 diabetes

Wang, Xiaowen · Xia, Pengfei · Wang, Fenglei · Wu, Zhiyuan · Hu, Yang · Eliassen, A. Heather · Zeleznik, Oana A. · Bhupathiraju, Shilpa N. · Mousavi, Seyed Mohammad · Prentice, Ross L. · Tinker, Lesley F. · Zheng, Cheng · Liu, Simin · Huang, Ying · Navarro, Sandi L. · Playdon, Mary C. · Moore, Steven C. · Snetselaar, Linda · Tabung, Fred K. · Manson, JoAnn E. · Qi, Lihong · Raftery, Daniel · Willett, Walter C. · Lampe, Johanna W. · Neuhouser, Marian L. · Sun, Qi Published 2026-01-15 DOI 10.1038/s44360-025-00023-8 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Plasma metabolomic signatures can objectively capture dietary carbohydrate intake from distinct food sources (whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes vs. added sugars, refined grains, potatoes) and are differentially associated with type 2 diabetes risk. Whole-grain, fruit, vegetable and legume signatures reduce T2D risk (HR 0.73–0.95 per s.d.), whereas added-sugar, refined-grain and potato signatures increase it (HR 1.07–1.36 per s.d.) — providing objective, biology-grounded surrogates for dietary assessment that sidestep recall bias inherent in questionnaires.

★

Why It Matters

  • Overcomes the core limitation of nutritional epidemiology. Dietary recall questionnaires (FFQs) carry well-known measurement errors. This study substitutes objectively measured blood metabolites as diet proxies, confirming and sharpening associations that FFQ-based cohort studies could only approximate.
  • Source-level resolution. Rather than treating "carbohydrates" as a monolith, the study demonstrates that metabolomic fingerprints differ radically across eight carbohydrate subcategories — fried potatoes, for example, carry an HR of 1.49 for T2D, while baked potatoes show no significant association (HR 1.01).
  • Three-phase design strengthens causal inference. Discovery (n = 1,196 with 7-day diet records), external replication in a controlled feeding study (n = 153), and prospective cohort validation (n = 11,454 with up to 30 years of follow-up) form a rigorous pipeline rarely achieved in nutritional metabolomics.
  • Actionable support for dietary guidelines. The biological pathway evidence (IPA, betaine, hippuric acid from plant sources; TAGs from refined carbs) provides mechanistic grounding for existing public-health recommendations to emphasize whole grains, fruits, vegetables and legumes.
  • Code is open. Analysis programs are publicly available at github.com/Xiaowen-bot/carb, enabling independent replication and extension.

Core Data — Sources & Volume

Discovery cohort
Lifestyle Validation Study (LVS) — 1,196 healthy participants (62.1% women, mean age 66.4 years) with 7-day diet records (7DDRs) and plasma metabolomics. Drawn from two sub-studies: Men's LVS (MLVS, 2011–2013, HPFS & Harvard Pilgrim Health Care) and Women's LVS (WLVS, 2010–2012, NHS & NHSII). A total of 293 named plasma metabolites were included after quality filtering.
External replication
Nutrition and Physical Activity Assessment Study Feeding Study (NPAAS-FS) within the Women's Health Initiative (WHI) — 153 postmenopausal women, 14-day controlled feeding study (2011–2013), serum metabolomics via Metabolon Q-exactive UPLC-MS/MS platform plus lipidomics (Sciex QTRAP 5500 Lipidyzer, 1,070 lipids in 13 lipid classes).
Prospective cohort
Nurses' Health Study (NHS), blood collected 1989–1990 from 32,826 participants; Nurses' Health Study II (NHSII), blood collected 1996–1999 from 29,611 participants; Health Professionals Follow-up Study (HPFS), blood collected 1993–1995 from 18,225 participants. After exclusions, 11,454 pooled for the T2D analysis, followed through June 2020.
Metabolomics platform
LVS: LC-MS at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard — HILIC-pos, HILIC-neg and C8-pos modes. NPAAS-FS: Metabolon platform (Metabolon Inc.) + Sciex QTRAP 5500 Lipidyzer (Northwest Metabolomics Research Center, University of Washington). Cross-platform mapping via HMDB numbers, metabolite names/synonyms, and Lipid Maps.
Dietary assessment
LVS: 7DDRs processed via Nutrition Data System for Research software (University of Minnesota, >150 nutrients/constituents). Cohorts: validated FFQs (NHS since 1984, NHSII since 1991, HPFS since 1986). NPAAS-FS: 14-day feeding menus converted to standard servings/day.
T2D ascertainment
Self-reported cases confirmed by supplemental questionnaire against ADA criteria; validation studies within NHS and HPFS confirmed diagnoses in 98% and 97% of participants, respectively.
Code availability
Analysis code: github.com/Xiaowen-bot/carb. Raw data available upon written request (controlled access via External Collaborators Committee for NHS/NHSII/HPFS; WHI P&P Committee for NPAAS-FS/WHI).

Core Methods

  • Elastic net regression with cross-validation: α = 0.5 (equal LASSO + Ridge mix), 10-fold cross-validation with leave-one-out, lambda selected by minimum MSE (R cv.glmnet). Applied to all 293 metabolites simultaneously to select and weight metabolites into composite indices for each carbohydrate category.
  • Metabolomic index construction: linear combination of selected metabolites weighted by elastic net coefficients — Metabolomic Index = Σ βi × Mi. Evaluated using Pearson correlation coefficients vs. 7DDR-assessed intake (Pearson r 0.33–0.65 in LVS).
  • Triad method validation: classic nutritional epidemiology triangulation between FFQ, 7DDR and metabolomic index to estimate validity coefficients for each instrument against "true" intake (r = 0.47–0.69).
  • Multivariable linear regression: volcano plots and Bonferroni-corrected screening (P < 0.05/293) of individual metabolites against each carbohydrate exposure variable, adjusted for age, BMI, ancestry, smoking, physical activity, alcohol, AHEI and total calorie intake.
  • Cox proportional hazards regression: prospective T2D risk estimation (person-time from blood draw to T2D diagnosis, death or June 2020). Model 2 adjusted for age, cohort, ancestry, fasting status, family history, smoking, alcohol, early-adulthood BMI, physical activity, hypertension, cholesterol, total calories and AHEI (all in quintiles or categories).
  • Metabolite Set Enrichment Analysis (MSEA): knowledge-based metabolite grouping (lipid classes, amino acids, etc.) to identify biologically coherent groups associated with T2D via enrichment scores from Cox-model estimates.
  • Sensitivity analyses: LASSO alternative, stability selection (80% threshold over 100 subsampling iterations), adjustment for self-reported diet, mediation analysis with bootstrapping (500 resamples), nested case–control within NHS (n = 1,456).

① Spatialize the metabolomic-diet-T2D pipeline: a geospatially aware nutritional risk score

Extending this paperThe paper builds metabolomic indices from demographic and dietary variables but treats all participants as spatially interchangeable — no geographic heterogeneity is modeled despite the participants being drawn from geographically distinct cohorts (NHS in Massachusetts, HPFS nationwide). A direct extension: attach residential ZIP code or county to each participant, link to food-environment data (food deserts, grocery-store density, fast-food proximity), and use geographically weighted regression (GWR) to estimate how the strength of the metabolite–T2D association varies spatially. This would reveal whether certain carbohydrate metabolomic signatures are amplified in food-desert neighborhoods and would add an environmental exposure layer the paper currently omits.

Added value to my researchThis is a direct application of my MGWR/GWR methods to nutritional epidemiology: treating the metabolomic index as the "outcome" and local food-environment features as spatially varying modifiers creates a health-geography bridge between omics and urban food access. Combined with my 2SFCA/R2SFCA toolkit, one could simultaneously estimate spatial accessibility to healthy food and its correspondence to metabolomic signatures — linking facility-location optimization to biological disease prevention.

② Replace elastic net with an interpretable, multi-source deep learning metabolomics encoder

Extending this paperThe paper's elastic net regression is a linear model that cannot capture non-linear interactions among the 293 metabolites or cross-platform heterogeneity (Broad LC-MS vs. Metabolon vs. Lipidyzer). A natural extension: train a cross-platform metabolomics encoder — a lightweight self-supervised model that embeds metabolite profiles from all three platforms into a common latent space, then fine-tunes downstream to predict carbohydrate exposure or T2D risk. The shared embedding would resolve the "platform-dependent differences" the authors identify as a current limitation, enabling the NPAAS-FS and NHS data to be co-analyzed without platform-by-platform metabolite matching.

Added value to my researchThis connects directly to my work on geospatial foundation models and multi-source data fusion: just as mobile-phone, remote-sensing and social-media data require cross-modality harmonization, metabolomics data from different analytical platforms require analogous embedding alignment. The techniques (contrastive pre-training, domain adaptation) are transferable across the geospatial and biomedical domains, and the explainability (SHAP/LIME attribution) would satisfy both clinical and spatial interpretation needs.

Cross-ref (corpus): Diet-omics in the Study of Urban and Rural Crohn disease Evolution (SOURCE) cohort — 10.1038/s41467-024-48106-6

③ Incorporate gut-microbiome mediators into the carbohydrate–metabolite–T2D pathway

Extending this paperThe paper acknowledges that IPA (3-indolepropionic acid), the strongest cross-carbohydrate metabolite, is produced through gut-microbiota fermentation of dietary fibre and tryptophan — yet the current dataset has no microbiome measurements. A logical extension: link this metabolomic framework to an available cohort with paired gut-microbiome (16S rRNA or metagenomics) and metabolomics data, and use mediation analysis to partition the total carbohydrate → metabolite → T2D pathway into a gut-mediated component and a direct metabolic component. This would sharpen both mechanistic interpretation and therapeutic target identification (e.g., probiotic or prebiotic interventions for the microbially-derived IPA arm).

Applying graph-based causal inference to multi-omics data is a natural extension of my agent-based and network-community-detection methods to biomedical networks.

④ Apply the metabolomic-signature validation pipeline to environmental exposome data

Extending this paperThe paper's three-phase discovery-replication-cohort pipeline is a reusable methodological template. It could be extended beyond dietary carbohydrates to validate environmental exposome signatures — e.g., constructing plasma metabolomic indices of air-pollution exposure (PM2.5, NO2) or residential green-space exposure, then examining their prospective associations with metabolic disease. Several participants in the NHS/HPFS cohorts live in geocoded locations for which satellite-derived pollution and greenness data exist, making this a feasible extension of the same dataset.

⑤ Translate the metabolomic carbohydrate quality index into a population-level screening tool with equity analysis

Extending this paperThe paper produces validated metabolomic indices of carbohydrate quality that outperform FFQ-based self-report. The next translational step: design a population-level metabolic screening program where a parsimonious panel of 5–10 blood metabolites (extracted from the paper's elastic-net coefficients) is measured alongside routine blood draws, producing a personalized "carbohydrate quality score" and a T2D risk stratum. Using the paper's HR estimates as calibration anchors, one could derive absolute-risk thresholds and compare cost-effectiveness of metabolomics-guided vs. questionnaire-guided prevention interventions — including equity analysis of who benefits most by geography and socio-economic status.

Added value to my researchThis directly links to my health-geography accessibility work: even if the metabolomic biomarker panel is validated, its clinical utility depends on who can access the bloodwork — spatial accessibility to diagnostic labs, insurance coverage, and digital-health literacy all mediate uptake. My 2SFCA spatial accessibility models and telehealth/digital-divide frameworks provide the machinery to map where screening would be under-utilized and where targeted outreach could achieve the greatest T2D prevention benefit.

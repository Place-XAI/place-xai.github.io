Comparative analysis of smart city scientific research trends in the USA and China

Yuan Lai · Haoxiang Zhao Published 2025-08-25 DOI 10.1038/s44284-025-00305-y Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Smart city research in the USA and China is simultaneously converging and diverging. Using large language models to parse funded research proposals from the NSF and the NSFC, the authors find shared scientific inquiries (sensing, data, infrastructure, mobility) but divergent national trajectories: a persistent tension between techno-centric priorities and human-oriented imperatives. Differences in funding mechanisms and national context shape distinct research pathways that, the authors warn, risk fragmenting the field further.

Core Methods

  • LLM-based proposal mining (SRPR workflow): large language models ingest funded NSF/NSFC proposal text and classify it into smart-city subjects/themes, normalizing heterogeneous, bilingual funding text into comparable categories (Fig. 5).
  • Comparative trend analysis: common features vs. divergent advancement of the two countries' research agendas are contrasted along technology-driven and human-centered dimensions (Fig. 1).
  • Subject-flow mapping: Sankey-style flows trace how proposals connect science → technology → real-world problems, exposing where the two systems align or split (Fig. 2).
  • Funding–context correlation: links funding patterns to local/national settings to test how mechanisms shape research priorities (Fig. 3).
  • Research-network analysis: characterizes the expansion and structure of smart-city research networks over time (Fig. 4).

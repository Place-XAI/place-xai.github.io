Urban meteorology–chemistry coupling in compound heat–ozone extremes

Xueyu Zhou · Mengmeng Li · Xin Huang · Tengyu Liu · Haoran Zhang · Ximeng Qi · Zilin Wang · Yue Qin · Guannan Geng · Jiaping Wang · Xuguang Chi · Aijun Ding Published 2025-08-15 DOI 10.1038/s44284-025-00302-1 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Heatwaves do not merely accelerate surface chemistry — they reorganize ozone pollution vertically. Using a tethered-airship campaign over Nanjing (197 vertical profiles to 1,200 m) plus meteorology–chemistry coupled modeling, the authors show that heatwave-reinforced turbulence lifts NOx from the surface into the upper boundary layer, shifting the photochemical regime from VOC-sensitive near the ground to NOx-sensitive aloft and accelerating O3 production both at the surface and aloft. Stringent NOx controls narrow this vertical disparity; emission cuts implied by China's carbon-neutrality pledge could cut warm-season MDA8 O3 under heatwaves by 41–47% across four megacity clusters, even as heatwaves intensify.

★

Why It Matters

  • Resolves a vertical blind spot. Almost all heat–O3 evidence is ground-based; the airship campaign delivers rare in-situ vertical profiles of O3, NOx and VOCs in the urban boundary layer, exposing a mechanism (turbulent NOx uplift) invisible at the surface.
  • Explains a "paradox" in the data. During heatwaves satellite tropospheric NO2 columns rise while surface NO2 falls — the paper shows this is the fingerprint of vertical precursor redistribution, not conflicting emissions.
  • Reframes the heatwave "penalty" as policy-contingent. Whether a hot day becomes a compound pollution hazard depends on the emission regime; deep NOx cuts can even flip the surface heatwave penalty into a reward (–12.9% O3 at 80% NOx reduction).
  • Ties air-quality co-benefits to carbon neutrality. It quantifies how China's net-zero pathway could push warm-season NO2 below the WHO guideline and drive heat–O3 co-occurrence toward <0.1 days yr-1 by 2060 — actionable for city climate-resilience planning.

Core Methods

  • Tethered-airship vertical sampling: high-time-resolution in-situ profiling of O3, NOx and VOCs to 1,200 m, paired with a ground supersite (SORPES) and ceilometer PBLH, to observe daytime evolution of precursor stratification under heat.
  • Relative heatwave definition: a heatwave day = Tmax above the 90th percentile from a 15-day moving window (1969–2019 ERA5 baseline; CMIP6 for the future); co-occurrence = heatwave day with MDA8 O3 > 160 µg m-3.
  • F0AM box model (MCM v3.3.1): observation-constrained 0-D photochemistry to build EKMA isopleths and locate the VOC- vs NOx-sensitive regime at surface and ~1 km.
  • WRF–Chem single-column model (SCM) with process diagnosis: six paired heatwave/normal experiments decomposing O3 change into vertical mixing (Met_O3Vmix), photochemical-rate acceleration (PhotChem_Rate), biogenic-VOC boost (Emis_BVOC) and precursor redistribution (Met_NOxVmix).
  • Regional WRF–Chem 2060 projections: 27-km China-wide warm-season runs (CB chemistry + MOSAIC aerosol, MEGAN biogenics) under baseline, NOx-only-cut and net-zero/clean-air scenarios to quantify future heat–O3 co-occurrence.
  • NOx-reduction sweep: stepwise emission cuts to locate tipping points of regime transition and O3 mitigation, separating "heatwave penalty" from "heatwave reward."

① Turn the single-city vertical mechanism into a national surrogate field

Extending this paperThe mechanism is established from one airship site (Nanjing) and a single-column model, then extrapolated to four clusters via expensive 27-km WRF–Chem runs. A direct extension: train an explainable ML / physics-informed emulator that maps (Tmax, PBLH, radiation, NOx/VOC fields) → vertical O3 production profile, using the paper's SCM ensemble as labels, so the heat–O3 coupling can be reconstructed for any city without a per-city chemical transport run.

Unveiling deployable rooftop solar potential across Chinese cities

Mai Shi · Xi Lu · Michael T. Craig Published 2025-07-14 DOI 10.1038/s44284-025-00270-6 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Across 367 Chinese cities, only about 42% of the national technical potential of rooftop photovoltaics (RPV) is realistically deployable once real-world electricity consumption and regulatory constraints are imposed. Accounting for these factors shifts the deployment focus from rural areas to urban public and industrial buildings, and from western to northern and central regions. To maximize value, the paper argues initial deployment should prioritize public and industrial buildings in central and southern cities — giving actionable, building-type-specific pathways toward China's RPV targets.

Why It Matters

  • Corrects a systematic over-estimate. Decades of RPV studies report technical or economic potential; this work shows that real consumption and regulation cut that figure by more than half (only ~42% deployable), so headline targets and grid plans built on technical potential are likely inflated.
  • Re-targets where panels should go. The constraints flip the priority from rural rooftops to urban public/industrial buildings and from western to northern/central regions — a concrete reallocation of subsidies, grid upgrades and siting effort.
  • Building-type granularity, not just totals. Distinguishing residential, public and industrial buildings (with block- and building-scale availability factors) makes the estimate usable for city-level planning rather than abstract national headlines.
  • Aligned with national policy. By ranking cities and building types against China's RPV deployment targets under economic-value vs carbon-reduction objectives, it offers a sequencing roadmap rather than a single number.

Core Methods

  • Rooftop area estimation: derive usable rooftop area on a 1 km² fishnet from built-up/impervious products, converting surface to rooftop via building-type, density and height relationships.
  • Availability factors: apply block-scale (BF) and building-scale (DF) availability factors (Extended Data Table 1) that down-weight raw rooftop area for shading, structure and usability by building type.
  • Technical-potential simulation: couple usable area with typical-meteorological-year weather files and a PV performance model to compute generation per cell and city.
  • Deployable filtering: impose real electricity consumption matching and regulatory constraints, collapsing technical potential to deployable potential (the ~42% national figure) and a city-level deployable-to-technical (and DPOD) ratio.
  • Pathway optimization: order RPV deployment across cities/building types to meet national targets under two objectives — maximizing economic value vs maximizing carbon reduction — yielding building-type-prioritized development pathways.

① Replace static availability factors with an explainable learned rooftop classifier

Extending this paperThe deployable estimate hinges on hand-set block- and building-scale availability factors (Extended Data Table 1) keyed to building type, density and height per 1 km² cell. These are coarse and city-invariant. A direct extension: train an explainable machine-learning rooftop classifier on high-resolution imagery/building footprints to predict usable-fraction per roof, with SHAP-style attribution exposing which morphological features drive deployability — turning the paper's fixed BF/DF table into a learned, auditable, city-specific surface.

③ Add the thermal/health side-effects the deployable model omits

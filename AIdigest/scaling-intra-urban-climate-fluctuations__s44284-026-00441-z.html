Scaling intra-urban climate fluctuations

Marc Duran-Sala · Martin Hendrick · Gabriele Manoli Published 2026-05-19 DOI 10.1038/s44284-026-00441-z Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Intra-urban variations of temperature and air pollution are not city-specific noise: across 142 cities worldwide, the full probability distributions of urban heat and PM fields collapse onto a small set of universal scaling functions inspired by finite-size scaling in statistical physics. A simple logarithmic relation ties these climate fields to street-network properties — so average network features (for example, intersection density) are sufficient to characterize the full variability of temperature and pollution both within and across cities, enabling climate information to enter reduced-complexity urban models.

★

Why It Matters

  • Cures the "city-boundary" disease of urban scaling. Classic scaling laws relate averages (mean temperature vs. population) and are notoriously sensitive to how city limits are drawn. This work shifts from averages to whole distributions, making intra-urban variability — not just the citywide mean — the object of a general law.
  • Imports physics into urban climate. Borrowing finite-size scaling from statistical physics gives a principled way to collapse heterogeneous cities of different sizes onto one master curve, turning case-by-case description into a transferable theory.
  • Makes morphology a predictor of climate. A single, interpretable logarithmic link between street-network structure and climate fields means planners can reason about heat and air quality from geometry they actually control.
  • Bridges to reduced-complexity models. Because the variability is captured by a few scaling functions, climate fields can be injected into fast urban-systems models without running full physical simulations for every city.

Core Methods

  • Finite-size scaling collapse: treat each city as a finite system and rescale its temperature/PM fields so that marginal and joint probability distributions from cities of different sizes collapse onto shared master functions.
  • Distributional (not mean-based) analysis: characterize the full intra-urban variability via marginal and joint distributions, rather than the single citywide average used in classical urban scaling.
  • Logarithmic morphology→climate relation: derive a compact log-relation linking street-network properties (for example, intersection density n) to temperature and air-pollution fields, showing average network features suffice to reconstruct variability.
  • Stochastic radial decay model (Fig. 4): a reduced-complexity generative model in which climate variables decay radially from the urban core with a stochastic component, reproducing observed distributions.
  • Cross-city generalization: validate that the same scaling functions hold within and across the 142-city global sample, supporting integration into reduced-complexity urban-systems models.

① From intersection density to a full street-network signature of climate

Extending this paperThe paper distills morphology to essentially a single scalar — average intersection density n — driving a logarithmic link to climate. A direct extension: feed the whole street graph (betweenness, orientation entropy, block-size distribution, sky-view proxies) into the scaling collapse and ask which network descriptors actually carry the temperature/PM signal, using explainable ML to rank them while preserving the finite-size-scaling backbone. This would test whether the universal collapse is robust to richer morphology or whether n is uniquely special.

Added value to my researchThis is squarely my explainable-ML-for-spatial-prediction line: a SHAP-ranked, network-aware model of urban heat/pollution exposure that can later weight accessibility catchments by environmental burden, not just travel time.

② Make the radial-decay model a physics-informed graph operator

Extending this paperThe paper's stochastic radial-decay model (Fig. 4) assumes monocentric decay from a single core — fine for compact cities, weak for polycentric ones. Extending it: replace the radial coordinate with a physics-informed graph neural operator on the street network whose smoothness/decay prior is the paper's own scaling function, so the model generalizes to multi-centre and irregular cities while keeping the universal collapse as a soft constraint.

Added value to my researchThis is exactly my physics-informed graph-learning agenda: encode a conservation/decay prior into a GNN so a climate-field emulator trained on a few hundred cities transfers to unseen ones — a reusable GeoAI environmental-exposure operator.

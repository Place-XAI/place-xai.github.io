A framework for risk–benefit analysis of vaccines approved through accelerated pathways

Bethan Cracknell Daniels · Timothy Endy · Joseph Lewnard · Jose Luis Di Fabio · Oscar Cortes-Azuero · J. Gabrielle Breugelmans · Simon Cauchemez · Alexander Precioso · Albert Ko · Rebecca E. Chandler · Stephen J. Thomas · Nicole Lurie · Henrik Salje Published 2026-01-15 DOI 10.1038/s44360-025-00027-4 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

When a vaccine is licensed through an accelerated pathway (no Phase III efficacy RCT), prior knowledge of the age-specific risk of disease given infection can still anchor a quantitative risk–benefit assessment. Applying a generalizable, pathogen-agnostic Bayesian framework to the chikungunya vaccine IXCHIQ, the authors find the benefit clearly outweighs the risk for adults aged 18–64 across all epidemiological settings, and a net benefit for those aged 65+ in large outbreaks (including La Réunion) and endemic settings over ten years — but insufficient evidence of net benefit in small outbreaks or for travellers. The framework also derives the maximum tolerable rate of vaccine-associated severe adverse events that still yields net benefit for a panel of pathogens.

Why It Matters

  • Decision tool for a live regulatory crisis. IXCHIQ vaccination in those aged 65+ was suspended after three deaths in La Réunion; the EMA later lifted restrictions while the FDA permanently suspended the licence. This framework supplies the transparent quantitative arithmetic that such divergent rulings demand.
  • Separates absolute benefit from strength of evidence. It reports both cases/deaths/DALYs averted and the posterior probability that benefit exceeds risk — exposing situations (e.g. 18–64-year mortality) where the absolute risk is tiny yet evidence can never reach 95% certainty without >100,000 vaccinees.
  • Generalizable beyond chikungunya. The same machinery yields acceptable SAE thresholds for Ebola, measles (pre- and post-vaccine era) and SARS-CoV-2 (2020 vs Omicron 2022), showing how tolerable risk shifts with attack rate and severity over time.
  • Actionable for trial design. It shows that enrolling ~4,000 adults aged 18–64 and ~1,000 aged 65+ would have demonstrated net benefit across scenarios — a concrete recruitment recommendation for high-risk subpopulations underrepresented in immunogenicity trials.

Core Methods

  • Deterministic risk model: severe outcomes by vaccination status from four factors — attack rate λ, age-specific severe-outcome-given-infection α, vaccine efficacy ν, and age-specific SAE risk δ; outcomes averted = S(no-vax) − S(vax).
  • Safety threshold derivation: the maximum acceptable vaccine-SAE probability is closed-form δmax ≤ α × λ × ν — the analytic core that yields tolerable-risk bounds per pathogen.
  • Bayesian probability of net benefit: conjugate Beta(1,1) priors on α and δ, binomial likelihoods → Beta posteriors; 100,000 posterior draws give the probability that δ ≤ δmax (i.e. that benefit beats risk).
  • Power / sample-size analysis: one-sided exact binomial test at 80% power to find the number of vaccinees needed to conclude net mortality benefit when zero deaths are observed.
  • DALY accounting: YLL + YLD using La Réunion life expectancy, 50% symptomatic, 13%/26% medically attended (18–64 / 65+), 50% of attended cases → 6-month chronic arthralgia (disability weight 0.233).
  • Sensitivity analyses: vaccine efficacy 0–100% (baseline 95%), attack rate 0–50%, 0/1/3 vaccine-linked deaths, and SAEs equated to chronic vs acute chikungunya.

① Make the attack rate spatial: a geo-resolved risk–benefit surface

Extending this paperThe framework hinges on a single scenario-level attack rate λ (5% / 30% / 2.4%/yr), yet the authors stress that the true attack rate is the most critical and most uncertain input. A natural extension is to replace the scalar λ with a spatially resolved attack-rate field estimated from fine-grained surveillance and serosurveys, producing a map of where net benefit holds within a territory like La Réunion rather than one verdict for the whole island. Fine-scale population density is a known predictor of arbovirus wave dynamics, so density-aware λ surfaces would sharpen the benefit boundary.

Added value to my researchThis is exactly my spatiotemporal-KDE and density-driven epidemiology turf: feeding a kernel-smoothed, density-weighted λ field into δmax = α·λ·ν turns the paper's table into a GeoAI decision layer for sub-national vaccine targeting — a public-health-policy product I can build with mobile-phone and RS-derived exposure proxies.

② Add an equity dimension to the benefit calculus

Extending this paperThe framework is explicitly an individual-level risk–benefit model and deliberately omits indirect effects and distributional concerns. Extending it: layer a population allocation problem on top — given scarce IXCHIQ doses and heterogeneous age/comorbidity/exposure, where do doses maximize averted DALYs and minimize inequity? The paper already shows benefit varies sharply by age and setting, so an equity-aware allocation is a direct, policy-relevant follow-on.

③ Couple the framework to a forecasting front-end for live outbreaks

Extending this paperThe authors note that in a new outbreak the attack rate is unknown, so decisions must rest on epidemic forecasting; assuming a high λ over-vaccinates if transmission fizzles, assuming low λ underuses an effective vaccine. The clean extension is to wire a probabilistic epidemic forecaster directly into the Bayesian benefit step, propagating forecast uncertainty in λ into the posterior probability of net benefit — turning a retrospective tool into a real-time decision engine.

Added value to my researchThis pairs my explainable-ML and physics-informed (SIR-constrained) spatial prediction with the paper's closed-form δmax: a hybrid where the learned λ forecaster is the data-driven half and the risk–benefit identity is the mechanistic half — a concrete physics-informed GeoAI pipeline for outbreak response.

④ Anchor the α (severity-given-infection) term in better serosurveillance

Extending this paperEvery conclusion rests on the age-specific severe-outcome-given-infection α, here estimated from a single Paraguay outbreak; the authors explicitly call for La Réunion serosurveys to validate transferability. Extending the paper means building a multi-outbreak hierarchical model of α that pools serosurvey-anchored IFRs across populations, with partial pooling so a new setting borrows strength while flagging when local α departs from the prior.

⑤ Embed risk–benefit verdicts within healthcare-access geography

Extending this paper"Medically attended chikungunya" — the paper's benefit endpoint — silently assumes care is reachable; in arbovirus-endemic regions with thin health infrastructure, that assumption breaks, biasing both α estimation and the realized benefit of averting a case. Extending the paper: condition the severe-outcome and DALY terms on spatial accessibility to care, so the framework reflects that an averted case is worth more where treatment access is poor, and that under-ascertainment distorts α where access is poor.

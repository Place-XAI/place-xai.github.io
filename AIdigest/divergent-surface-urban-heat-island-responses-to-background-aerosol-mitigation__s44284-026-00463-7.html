Divergent surface urban heat island responses to background aerosol mitigation

Liu, Shu · Zhou, Yuyu · Zhao, Lei · Mills, Gerald · Li, Xinchang "Cathy" · Zhang, Hongsheng · Seto, Karen C. · Gong, Peng Published 2026-06-19 DOI 10.1038/s44284-026-00463-7 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Background aerosol mitigation produces divergent surface urban heat island (UHI) responses depending on hydroclimate: aerosol reductions amplify warm-season surface UHI in humid climates but weaken it in arid regions. The mechanism is asymmetric evaporative cooling — in humid climates, increased solar radiation boosts rural vegetation and rainfall, widening the urban–rural temperature gap; in arid climates, reduced rainfall suppresses rural evapotranspiration more than urban, narrowing the gap. These hydroclimate-dependent responses call for prioritizing urban heat mitigation in humid regions as air-quality policies continue to cut aerosol emissions globally.

Why It Matters

  • Reveals an unintended consequence of clean-air policy. Countries that have successfully reduced aerosol emissions — notably China's Air Pollution Prevention and Control Action Plan (APPCAP, 2013–2017) — may inadvertently be intensifying urban heat hazards in humid regions, creating a compounded risk for city dwellers.
  • Resolves a globally unexplored coupling. While aerosol–UHI co-occurrence has been documented observationally, no prior study had isolated the causal effect of background aerosol changes on UHI using large-ensemble climate simulations that separate aerosol forcing from other drivers.
  • Produces a physically grounded, climate-regime-dependent framework. The hydroclimate lens (humid vs. arid) provides a transferable rule for planners: the same aerosol reduction policy has opposite UHI consequences depending on local water availability.
  • Directly informs urban adaptation priorities. By projecting that rapid urbanization combined with continued aerosol reduction will disproportionately raise urban warming in humid regions, the paper identifies where urban greening, cool-surface, and other heat-mitigation investments are most urgently needed.
  • Validated with observational data. CESM1 large-ensemble results are cross-checked against satellite AOD trends (LGHAP v2) and 1 km daily land surface temperature observations (2003–2020), strengthening causal inference beyond pure simulation.

Core Methods

  • Large-ensemble causal attribution: compare 20 CESM1 "XAER" (aerosol-only forcing) runs against 20 "ALL" (all-forcing) runs to isolate the aerosol signal from greenhouse gas and other influences; delta UHI = XAER difference in urban–rural surface temperature.
  • Surface energy balance decomposition: decompose UHI changes (δΔTs) into contributions from downward solar radiation (δSin), albedo contrast (δΔα), anthropogenic heat (δΔQah), sensible heat (δΔSH), latent heat (δΔLH), and ground heat storage (δΔG) — summing components to verify closure.
  • Hydroclimate regime classification: define humid/intermediate/arid zones using three alternative metrics (precipitation, vapor pressure deficit, soil moisture) and test robustness of the divergent-response signal across all three classifications (Extended Data Fig. 1).
  • Regional case studies: deep-dive diagnostic analysis in East Asia (Fig. 2) and South Asia (Extended Data Fig. 3) to trace the specific moisture–vegetation–evapotranspiration pathways mediating the UHI response.
  • Observational cross-validation: compare CESM1-simulated AOD trends to LGHAP v2 satellite observations and simulated UHI trends to 1 km daily LST data over China during the APPCAP period (2013–2017), using linear trend analysis.
  • Statistical significance testing: two-sided Student's t-test (p < 0.05) on ensemble-mean differences; error bars represent mean ± 2 standard errors across the 20 members.
  • Analysis tools: NCAR Command Language (NCL) v6.6.2 and MATLAB R2022b; CESM source code publicly available at GitHub/ESCOMP/CESM.

Extending this paperThe paper focuses on warm-season mean UHI changes under aerosol forcing.

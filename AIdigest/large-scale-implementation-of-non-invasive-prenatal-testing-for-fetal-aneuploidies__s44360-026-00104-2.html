Large-scale implementation of non-invasive prenatal testing for fetal aneuploidies

Liu, Jing · Wu, Meng · He, Sijie · Yang, Linfeng · Zhao, Jianhong · Chen, Yingyao · Tang, Longmei · Zhao, Can · Li, Yali · Hao, Guimin · Zhou, Yingjie · Wang, Yunfang · Huo, Qingyun · Jiao, Hongyan · Zhang, Yuhua · Du, Runxuan · Tao, Defeng · Wang, Yuzhen · Chen, Lixia · Guo, Chang · Liu, Haiyan · Xing, Huanxia · Yang, Bo · Gao, Shuyang · Zhang, Jianguo · Zhao, Lijian · Gao, Jian · Zhu, Baosheng · Zhu, Jun Published 2026-04-06 DOI 10.1038/s44360-026-00104-2 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

A fully publicly funded, first-tier NIPT programme covering 1,185,416 pregnant women in Hebei province, China (2019–2022) achieved 93.3% population coverage and demonstrated NIPT's feasibility at provincial scale. NIPT reduced invasive prenatal diagnosis procedures by 97.4% compared to traditional maternal serum screening at better cost-effectiveness. Critically, NIPT-positive results — even false positives — identify pregnancies at significantly elevated risk for adverse perinatal outcomes beyond chromosomal anomalies, suggesting NIPT signals a broader indicator of pregnancy vulnerability.

Why It Matters

  • Largest real-world, population-level NIPT evaluation reported to date. With 1,185,416 participants and 93.3% provincial coverage over three years, this study provides the most comprehensive empirical evidence yet for first-tier NIPT feasibility and outcomes.
  • Quantifies epidemiology of all major aneuploidies simultaneously. Population-level incidence rates for trisomies 21, 18, 13 and all major sex chromosome aneuploidies in a single cohort — filling a gap in real-world prevalence data for non-trisomy 21 conditions.
  • Reveals NIPT as a broader perinatal risk flag, not just a chromosome detector. NIPT false-positive cases carried a 49% excess risk of small-for-gestational-age/preterm/low-birth-weight newborns; high-risk cases without confirmatory diagnosis had 85% excess risk — extending NIPT's clinical utility beyond its conventional scope.
  • Demonstrates equity-enhancing potential of public funding. By making NIPT fully publicly funded, Hebei province eliminated cost barriers and achieved near-universal uptake across urban–rural and socioeconomic strata, offering a blueprint for low- and middle-income settings.
  • Provides cost-effectiveness evidence for health-system decision-makers. NIPT as first-tier screening dominates maternal serum screening on both clinical outcomes and total programme cost — critical data for policy transitions at national scale.

Core Methods

  • Programme coverage calculation: NIPT uptake rate = NIPT tests performed ÷ total registered pregnancies in Hebei, stratified by city-level GDP-based socioeconomic tier and calendar period.
  • Diagnostic performance evaluation: sensitivity, specificity, positive predictive value (PPV) and estimated incidence calculated for each aneuploidy type, subdivided by maternal age and conception mode (natural vs. IVF).
  • Logistic regression / risk modelling: parental advanced age (≥35 years) as binary exposure; odds ratio for trisomy 21 risk (reported: 4.31-fold) estimated with multivariable adjustment (exact covariates not detailed in available text).
  • Relative risk (RR) analysis: RRs for adverse neonatal outcomes (small-for-gestational-age, preterm birth, low birth weight) estimated in NIPT false-positive and high-risk-without-PD groups vs. screen-negative reference; 95% confidence intervals reported.
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis: incremental cost-effectiveness of first-tier NIPT vs. MSS calculated from programme cost data and downstream health outcomes; PD reduction used as a primary efficiency metric.
  • Spatial stratification: geographic heterogeneity of NIPT coverage and PD rates across city socioeconomic tiers visualized and compared (Extended Data Fig. 1).

① Map the spatial accessibility of NIPT services to explain residual geographic inequity

Extending this paperThe paper stratifies NIPT coverage by city-level GDP but does not model the underlying supply-side driver: how far pregnant women must travel to access an NIPT-capable facility, and how this interacts with road network quality and rural-urban gradients. A direct extension would apply two-step floating catchment area (2SFCA) or R2SFCA methods to NIPT clinic locations across Hebei's prefectures — computing facility-to-population access ratios that isolate whether coverage gaps arise from affordability, awareness, or physical spatial accessibility.

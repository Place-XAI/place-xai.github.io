Molecular deep learning at the edge of chemical space

Derek van Tilborg · Luke Rossen · Francesca Grisoni Published 2026-04-22 DOI 10.1038/s42256-026-01216-w Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Molecular machine learning models routinely fail when predicting bioactivity for structurally out-of-distribution (OOD) molecules — precisely the novel candidates most valuable for drug discovery. This paper introduces the Joint Molecular Model (JMM), which simultaneously performs property prediction and molecular reconstruction, deriving a new metric called "unfamiliarity" from the reconstruction loss. Across 33 bioactivity datasets and 1.4 million commercially available screening compounds, unfamiliarity reliably detects distribution shifts that Bayesian uncertainty estimation misses. Prospective wet-lab validation targeting PIM1 and CDK1 kinases identified seven compounds with low micromolar potency, all structurally distant from training data (max Tanimoto similarity ≤ 0.38), demonstrating that unfamiliarity can reliably guide discovery beyond the charted edge of chemical space.

Why It Matters

  • Solves a fundamental gap in molecular AI reliability. Standard Bayesian uncertainty estimation becomes overconfident precisely when it matters most — on OOD molecules. Unfamiliarity provides a complementary, model-internal signal that remains meaningful under strong distribution shifts (KS statistic D = 0.999 for screening library vs. in-distribution test, versus D = 0.181 for uncertainty).
  • Bridges generative and predictive modelling. By jointly training a SMILES autoencoder alongside a bioactivity classifier, the paper repurposes reconstruction quality as a principled OOD detector — a conceptual shift from "how uncertain is my prediction" to "how familiar is this molecule to the model."
  • Systematic benchmark across 33 datasets and 1.4M molecules. The analysis spans 33 experimentally annotated bioactivity datasets (14,081 molecules per in-distribution/OOD split) and a combined 1,395,422-compound commercial screening library, giving statistical robustness far beyond single case-studies.
  • Experimentally validated in the wet lab. Seven compounds with confirmed low micromolar potency were identified for two clinically relevant kinases (PIM1 hit rate 17%, CDK1 7%), exceeding typical kinase screening benchmarks of 0.1–5%.
  • Open and reproducible. Processed datasets and results deposited at Zenodo and figshare; code and R visualization scripts published on GitHub — enabling immediate replication and extension by the community.

Core Methods

  • Joint Molecular Model (JMM): a semi-supervised autoencoder that simultaneously (a) encodes SMILES strings via a 1D-CNN into a 128-dimensional latent vector z, (b) decodes z back to SMILES using a conditioned LSTM (trained without teacher forcing), and (c) predicts bioactivity from z with an approximate Bayesian MLP classifier (anchored ensemble, M = 10 models). Joint loss: LJMM = Lreconstruction + 0.1 × LMLP.
  • Unfamiliarity metric (U): U(x) = log(Lreconstruction(x)) — lower reconstruction capability signals higher unfamiliarity. Captures OOD-ness in a model-internal, structure-aware way, independent of Bayesian uncertainty H(y|x) (Spearman r = −0.03 on 1.4M library molecules).
  • Spectral clustering for OOD data splits: Tanimoto similarity matrix on ECFPs of molecular cyclic skeletons is eigendecomposed; the most distant clusters (~25% of data) form the OOD test set — inducing realistic structural distribution shifts across all 33 benchmarks.
  • Multi-objective virtual screening: molecules ranked by distance to a utopia point balancing predicted bioactivity E(y|x), uncertainty H(y|x), and unfamiliarity U(x); three head-to-head selection strategies (A: low-U high-H; B: low-U low-H; C: high-U low-H) tested prospectively in the wet lab.
  • Baseline comparisons: Random Forest (ECFPs and CATS descriptors), MLP (ECFPs and SMILES encoder), embedding Mahalanobis distance, and similarity-based applicability domain — benchmarked on balanced accuracy, hit rate, and precision using 10-fold Monte Carlo cross-validation.
  • Biological assay: ADP-Glo Kinase Assay (Promega), 10 µM single-concentration point screen (n = 3 technical replicates), followed by 8-point dose–response curves for the 6 most active compounds per target; IC50 determined from sigmoidal fits.

① Transfer unfamiliarity-based OOD detection to geospatial prediction models

Extending this paperThe JMM's unfamiliarity metric was designed for discrete SMILES strings, but the core insight — that a jointly trained autoencoder's reconstruction loss encodes distributional distance better than Bayesian uncertainty — is domain-agnostic. A direct extension would apply the same joint-modelling principle to spatially structured tabular inputs (e.g., grid cells described by socioeconomic or environmental features), pretraining a spatial autoencoder on massive unlabelled census or remote-sensing tiles and jointly finetuning it for a specific prediction target. This would yield a spatial "unfamiliarity" score flagging locations outside the model's learned distribution — structurally identical to the molecular OOD challenge the paper solves, and untested in any spatial domain.

Added value to my researchMy explainable ML models for spatial prediction (health outcomes, telehealth utilization, housing prices) are routinely deployed to geographies outside their training regions. An unfamiliarity-inspired OOD detector, trained as a spatial reconstruction auxiliary task, would flag where my predictions are unreliable before policy-makers act on them. This directly extends my geospatial foundation-model fine-tuning agenda: pretrain a spatiotemporal autoencoder on satellite/census tiles, finetune jointly for accessibility or health outcome prediction, and use reconstruction loss as a spatial reliability surface.

② Generalize unfamiliarity from binary classification to regression and continuous uncertainty maps

Extending this paperThe paper demonstrates unfamiliarity as a reliability metric for binary bioactivity classification. A natural extension within the same molecular domain would generalize JMM to regression tasks (predicting IC50 values directly) and test whether unfamiliarity remains an equally strong performance predictor when label space is continuous. Table 2's metrics (balanced accuracy, hit rate, precision) are classification-specific; extending to mean absolute error and Spearman correlation for potency regression would widen the framework's applicability. Coupling reconstruction loss to a deep Gaussian process head would let unfamiliarity guide prospective active learning for potency optimization, not just hit identification.

③ Use unfamiliarity to guide iterative active learning for healthcare facility siting in underserved areas

Extending this paperThe paper explicitly notes that "unfamiliarity could assist in guiding reinforcement learning applications or extend beyond 'one-shot' virtual screening to iterative active learning." A concrete extension: replace the single-round utopia-point selection (Fig. 4) with an active learning loop in which unfamiliarity governs exploration (high-U = structurally novel regions worth probing) while uncertainty governs exploitation (low-H = zones of reliable prediction). Batch-mode selection with inter-candidate diversity penalties would further align with Bayesian optimization. Testing this loop against the current prospective kinase screening on a larger compound library would quantify the sample-efficiency gain over one-shot selection.

④ Apply spectral-clustering OOD splits to temporal concept drift in spatiotemporal prediction

Extending this paperThe paper's spectral-clustering OOD split protocol is the methodological backbone of its benchmark. An extension would ask: does the unfamiliarity metric's predictive power hold when distribution shifts are temporal rather than structural — e.g., training on pre-2020 bioactivity data and testing on post-2020 compounds where a new scaffold class emerged? A time-stratified benchmark alongside the structural benchmark would reveal whether unfamiliarity detects concept drift in addition to covariate shift, broadening its claimed generalizability and making it actionable for production ML systems that retrain on rolling time windows.

⑤ Build an "urban unfamiliarity" layer combining spatial autoencoder reconstruction loss with multi-source data entropy

Extending this paperThe paper maps unfamiliarity against uncertainty in 2D scatter plots (Figs. 3d–e) to identify utopia-point candidates. A richer extension would use the unfamiliarity landscape to perform chemical space cartography: embed the full 1.4M-compound library via UMAP colored by per-target unfamiliarity, then overlay experimental hit annotations to learn which unfamiliar-space regions are productively novel versus simply unrelated to the target. This would convert the paper's screening tool into a generative hypothesis engine for where next-round synthesis should focus — and how unfamiliarity gradients can guide scaffold-hopping campaigns systematically rather than heuristically.

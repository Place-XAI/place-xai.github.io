Machine learning-guided engineering of genetically encoded fluorescent calcium indicators

Sarah J. Wait · Marc Expòsit · Sophia Lin · Michael Rappleye · Justin Daho Lee · Samuel A. Core Conclusion

A stacked ensemble of three regression models — trained on 1,078 experimentally characterized GCaMP mutants and encoded with biophysical amino acid properties — screened 1,423 novel variants in silico and identified three new calcium indicators (eGCaMP, eGCaMP+, eGCaMP2+). The best variant, eGCaMP2+, achieves a 2.5-fold larger fluorescence response than prior fast indicators and outperforms sixth-, seventh-, and eighth-generation GCaMPs, demonstrating that small, curated sequence–function libraries can drive efficient protein engineering when paired with ensemble ML and domain-principled feature encoding.

Why It Matters

  • Compresses the protein engineering cycle. Rather than exhaustive trial-and-error mutagenesis, the ensemble reduced in-vitro testing from 1,423 candidates to 17 prioritized variants, with most predictions confirmed experimentally (accuracy 0.75 for kinetics, 0.56 for ΔF/F0).
  • Delivers practically superior biosensors. eGCaMP2+ (jGCaMP7s L317H/Q305D) achieves ~1,938% ΔF/F0 at KCl saturation — roughly twofold larger than GCaMP6s — while eGCaMP+ is the fastest-decaying variant tested (46% faster than jGCaMP8f), enabling resolution of rapid neural events in vivo.
  • Structure-agnostic approach is broadly transferable. Because the pipeline relies solely on sequence–function data (no 3D structural input required), it can be applied to any genetically encoded indicator or protein with a quantifiable readout and an existing mutational library.
  • Validates ensemble learning on small biological datasets. The approach overcomes data scarcity (~1,000 training examples) by combining diverse encodings and model architectures, achieving cross-validation R2 > 0.80 for both targets.
  • Provides open, reproducible infrastructure. All datasets are deposited on figshare, code on Zenodo and CodeOcean, crystal structure via RCSB, and plasmids via Addgene — enabling immediate reuse and iterative retraining by the community.

Core Methods

  • Biophysical feature encoding: each GCaMP sequence position is replaced by the amino acid’s quantitative value from an AAindex property matrix (hydrophobicity, polarity, folding energetics, etc.), yielding ~20% higher cross-validation R2 compared with one-hot or label encoding.
  • Stacked ensemble regression: three regressor architectures — Random Forest Regressor (RFR), K-Neighbors Regressor (KNR), and Multi-Layer Perceptron Regressor (MPNR) — each trained across the top-5 AAindex properties, producing 15 contributor models per target (ΔF/F0 or τ1/2); final prediction is the mean of all 15 outputs.
  • Hyperparameter optimization: grid-search tuning per model type, with SelectKBest feature selection and 80/20 train/test split (random seed 42). Ensemble R2 > 0.80 on held-out test set for both targets, exceeding any individual contributor model.
  • In silico volcano screening: for each of 1,423 novel variants, a two-sided unpaired t-test between the 15 contributor predictions for that variant and those for jGCaMP7s generates a predicted effect size and confidence score; impactful residues defined as top/bottom 2.5% of ranked predictions (±3σ).
  • PCA + K-means clustering: principal component analysis (elbow method for component selection) and K-means clustering characterize the variant library’s sequence space.
  • Multi-stage experimental validation: HEK293 acetylcholine assay (intermediate throughput) → primary rat cortical neuron electrical field stimulation (1/10/80 APs) → in vivo mouse fiber photometry (mPFC–NAc circuit, foot-shock paradigm); fluorescence analysis via the custom FUSE platform with Cellpose segmentation.

① Replace single-point saturation with deep-learning sequence design to escape local optima

Extending this paperThe paper deliberately restricts its novel library to single-point mutations at 75 known residue positions to avoid epistasis in a small training set. A direct extension: once the ensemble identifies impactful residues (L317, L302, Q305), retrain it on the combinatorial mutants generated in Fig. 4, then seed a deep-learning sequence design model to propose multi-point sequences that simultaneously optimize ΔF/F0, kinetics, and baseline fluorescence. This converts the paper’s conservative screen into an iterative active-learning loop — propose → test → retrain — sampling exponentially larger sequence space without proportionally increasing experimental cost.

Added value to my researchThe iterative retrain paradigm mirrors how I approach geospatial foundation model fine-tuning: a small labeled set seeds a pretrained backbone, predictions on unlabeled space identify high-value samples, and retraining closes the loop. This paper’s sequence-to-function workflow is an isomorphic case study for spatiotemporal prediction tasks where labeled data are scarce but proxy signals (like AAindex for amino acids, or built-environment indices for cities) are rich — a template for feature-encoding strategies in data-sparse GeoAI applications.

A foundation model for breast and lung cancer screening using non-contrast computed tomography

Zhiying Liang · Qingliang Niu · Jinmei Wang · Chunguang Han · Qin Li · Yiming Wu · Baoxi Zhu · Xipeng Han · Zhaorui Wang · Xia Wang · Chenglu Hu · Chongyan Liu · Yu Zhao · Jingjing Wang · Zikang Wang · Yongyi Ni · Jing Pei · Xuejun Qian Published 2026-02-05 DOI 10.1038/s44360-026-00055-8 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

OMAFound (carcinOMA Finder foundation) is a 3D self-supervised CT foundation model that performs simultaneous multi-cancer screening — breast and lung — from a single non-contrast (low-dose) CT, shifting screening from "one test for one cancer" to "one test for multi-cancer." Across 325,197 CT volumes from 151,386 patients (10 datasets) and a prospective 21,601-patient cohort, it reached balanced accuracy of 82.2% (breast) and 88.0% (lung) in females and 86.1% (lung) in males, matching mammography- and lung-specific baselines. When used as an aid, it raised 7 generalist radiologists' sensitivity by +38.9% (breast), +16.0% (lung) and +21.3% (patient-level) with no loss of specificity.

★

Why It Matters

  • Breaks the "single test for one cancer" paradigm. One inexpensive, widely available low-dose CT is repurposed to screen the two most common cancers at once, cutting out-of-pocket cost, cumulative radiation, and visit time — especially valuable in low-resource regions.
  • Opportunistic breast screening from chest CT. Chest CT is not a standard breast modality, yet the CT-based breast module reaches accuracy comparable to mammography (and higher sensitivity), so women already getting lung screening could be screened for breast cancer "for free."
  • Real prospective validation, not just retrospective AUC. A pre-registered 4-centre, 21,601-participant prospective study (ChiCTR2400081249) tests the model in the actual screening population it targets.
  • Measurable clinical lift for radiologists. The AI-assisted reader study quantifies sensitivity gains — largest for junior readers and for hard-to-spot breast lesions on CT — without sacrificing specificity.
  • Patient-level reasoning, not just organ-by-organ. An integration strategy encodes the clinical prior that synchronous primary cancers are rare, suppressing the false positives that accumulate when independent organ models are simply combined.

Core Methods

  • 3D self-supervised foundation model: a SwinUNETR-V2 encoder (residual conv + shifted-window self-attention) pretrained task-agnostically on 209,461 unlabelled CT volumes via combined rotation, reconstruction and contrastive losses.
  • Weakly supervised fine-tuning: organ-specific breast and lung modules use only patient-level labels (no boxes/masks); 3D adaptive pooling → fully connected → softmax for malignancy risk.
  • Patient-level fusion (Strategy 3): integrates Pbreast, Plung and an end-to-end fusion module (multi-window features + learnable class token + transformer) encoding the prior that synchronous primary cancers are rare — beating "noisy-or" and naive fusion.
  • Sex-stratified 3-phase screening: phase 1 splits by sex; phase 2 runs organ modules (lung only for males); phase 3 applies the fusion integration for females.
  • Mammography benchmark: a BMU-Net derivative (ResNet-18 + transformer, Mirai-initialized) on bilateral CC/MLO views, validated against 5 breast radiologists, to fairly compare CT-based breast screening.
  • Interpretability: five post hoc methods compared (Grad-CAM, Grad-CAM++, Layer-CAM, Finer-CAM, attention-based GMAR); Finer-CAM chosen by radiologist majority vote.
  • Evaluation & stats: weighted F1, balanced accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, AUC-ROC, C-index; 1,000-resample bootstrap + Wilson CIs; DeLong's test for AUC comparison.

A direct extension: wrap the model in a health-economic and geographic-access analysis — given the existing distribution of CT scanners and screening centres, estimate which populations gain the most preventive value per dollar and how opportunistic breast screening narrows access gaps.

④ Stress-test foundation-model reliability under distribution shift

Extending this paperOMAFound generalizes well on the tested cohorts, but foundation models violate classic ML reliability assumptions; external lung AUC already dropped on the non-screening PublicX set. Extending it: build a reliability protocol — calibration, selective prediction with abstention, and out-of-distribution detection — so the system flags when an unfamiliar scanner or rare presentation makes its score untrustworthy.

Experiments implementing small commuting models lack gravitational features

Bryce Kobrin · Thomas Schuster · Norman Y. Yao Published 2025-07-23 DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08939-7 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Small quantum circuits used in previous wormhole-teleportation experiments lack the essential gravitational features — thermalization, a correct teleportation signal, and perfect size winding — that would be required to interpret them as genuine holographic traversable wormholes. The paper demonstrates three concrete failures in "Model 1" (the circuit from Jafferis et al. 2022): it does not thermalize, its teleportation signal is inconsistent outside the training-data regime, and non-commuting extensions fail to show perfect size winding. These results establish that passing a few learned observables is insufficient evidence for gravitational dynamics when the underlying model is too small to possess the correct holographic structure.

Core Methods

  • Thermalization test (Fig. 1): Analyze energy-level spacing statistics of Model 1's Hamiltonian — a Wigner-Dyson / Poisson distribution comparison distinguishes chaotic (thermalizing) from integrable (non-thermalizing) systems. The paper shows Model 1 is integrable and therefore fails the necessary thermalization condition for a holographic wormhole.
  • Teleportation signal consistency (Fig. 2): Compute the mutual information / teleportation signal as a function of the coupling parameter g for Model 1, and compare behavior inside the ML training regime versus beyond it. Inconsistencies outside training data indicate the signal is an artifact of overfitting rather than an underlying gravitational mechanism.
  • Size winding analysis (Fig. 3): Examine the operator size distribution in the complex plane for non-commuting extensions of Model 1. Perfect size winding — a necessary holographic signature — is shown to be absent, ruling out the gravitational interpretation for the broader model family.
  • Analytical / numerical exact diagonalization: Given the small system size (7 qubits), exact diagonalization is feasible; results are sharp and model-specific rather than statistical estimates.
  • Theoretical framing: All diagnostics are grounded in the AdS/CFT correspondence and SYK holography literature — the criteria are not invented for this paper but are accepted standards in quantum gravity phenomenology.

④ Non-commutativity as a structural complexity requirement: lessons for graph neural network design

Extending this paperA central argument of the paper is that commutativity of the operator algebra is the fatal structural limitation of Model 1 — commuting Hamiltonians are integrable by definition and cannot scramble information holographically. The paper's extension would be to characterize exactly how much non-commutativity (in terms of operator norm of commutators) is required to achieve thermalization at finite N, producing a tractable design criterion for future experiments.

Added value to my researchIn my physics-informed graph learning work, the analog of non-commutativity is asymmetry in spatial interactions — origin-destination flows are not symmetric (commuting), and models that impose symmetric kernels (e.g., standard GCN with symmetric adjacency) may systematically miss directional spatial structure, just as commuting models miss holographic scrambling. The paper's argument motivates using directed/asymmetric graph operators in GNNs for urban mobility, and testing whether symmetric-kernel models fail out-of-distribution tests in a manner analogous to Fig. 2.

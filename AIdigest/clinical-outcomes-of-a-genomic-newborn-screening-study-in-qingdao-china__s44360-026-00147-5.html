Clinical outcomes of a genomic newborn screening study in Qingdao, China

Chen, Songchang · Huang, Hui · Chen, Yanping · Du, Peina · Wang, Xiaohong · Zhang, Jinglan · Chen, Xu · Fu, Qinmei · Zhao, Jingyu · Chen, Dongna · Sun, Yingmei · Zhou, Yingchao · Shang, Jianchun · Cui, Ruijuan · Yuan, Lili · Duan, Yong · Li, Lianghui · Fang, Mingyan · Zhang, Guohong · Huang, Yushan · Li, Nan · Shao, Zenghua · Deng, Bing · Wang, Mengmeng · Liu, Shanshan · Song, Lijie · Wei, Xiaoming · Wang, Shunyao · Yuan, Ke · Li, Dan · Ma, Di · Li, Hao · Chen, Lei · Xiang, Jiale · Peng, Zhiyu · Gao, Ya · Peng, Huanhuan · Chen, Fang · Jin, Xin · Liu, Junnian · Du, Yutao · Xu, Chenming · Zhao, Lijian · Pan, Silin Published 2026-06-03 DOI 10.1038/s44360-026-00147-5 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

A prospective whole-genome sequencing (WGS) screen of 9,992 newborns in Qingdao's West Coast New District found a 2.7% positive rate and clinically confirmed 19 cases of monogenic disease — of which 8 were missed entirely by traditional biochemical/auditory screening. Reanalysis of 19 symptomatic infants revealed pathogenic variants in 42.1% of cases. Integrating WGS into routine neonatal screening can substantially improve early detection of monogenic diseases in China, and the nationwide allele-frequency data (95.86 million variants) have been deposited as an open resource.

★

Why It Matters

  • Closes the detection gap of traditional NBS. Eight of 19 confirmed monogenic disease cases were invisible to conventional biochemical screening (MS/MS) and auditory screening (OAE/AABR), validating WGS as a complementary first-line screen rather than merely a confirmatory tool.
  • First prospective WGS-NBS cohort from mainland China at this scale. The QDnewborn study provides real-world clinical outcomes data (not just sequencing feasibility) for 9,992 consecutive newborns under a mandatory 3-day enrollment window.
  • Generates a population-level allele-frequency reference for Chinese newborns. The 95.86 million variants deposited in the Genome Variation Map (GVM000685) create a reusable public resource for variant pathogenicity interpretation in Chinese populations — a group underrepresented in global databases.
  • Integrates metabolomics with genomics. Untargeted blood-spot metabolomics (1,321 metabolites) provides a biochemical phenotype layer alongside WGS genotypes, enabling richer disease confirmation and opening a multi-omics path for neonatal screening.
  • Directly informs national policy. China's mandatory newborn screening panels are government-regulated; this study — funded under the National Key R&D Program — provides the clinical-outcomes evidence needed to update national guidelines toward genomic-first screening.

Core Methods

  • Prospective cohort design: enrollment of consecutive newborns within 3 days of birth (West Coast New District, Qingdao); longitudinal clinical follow-up to 31 March 2025 for positive WGS cases.
  • Whole-genome sequencing pipeline: BGI DNBSEQ platform → SOAPnuke quality control → BWA short-read alignment → GATK variant calling (HaplotypeCaller); SNP/INDEL genotyping across the full genome.
  • Variant annotation and pathogenicity classification: 222 disease genes and 214 genes screened; variants classified per ACMG/AMP 2015 criteria with OMIM disease references. Sanger sequencing used to confirm de novo variants (Extended Data Fig. 2).
  • Parallel traditional NBS: MALDI-TOF-MS hearing-loss gene panel (20 variants, 4 genes) and MS/MS metabolic disease screen (48 conditions) run on the same cohort; head-to-head comparison with WGS performed in Fig. 3.
  • Untargeted blood-spot metabolomics: 1,321 metabolites quantified; PLS-DA (partial least squares discriminant analysis) for group separation; KEGG pathway enrichment to link dyslipidaemia-related gene mutations to altered lipid biosynthesis metabolites (Fig. 4).
  • Reanalysis of symptomatic infants: 19 infants who developed symptoms underwent post-hoc WGS reanalysis and clinical confirmation; 8/19 (42.1%) had pathogenic/likely-pathogenic variants matching phenotype.

This would identify which sub-districts are underserved and where genomic screening programs should be prioritized if expanded citywide.

② Human-AI collaboration pipeline for automated variant prioritization in NBS

Extending this paperThe paper's variant classification step is performed by clinical geneticists following ACMG/AMP guidelines — a resource-intensive bottleneck that limits scalability. The paper's Supplementary Table 8 lists all P/LP variants identified; a natural extension is training an explainable ML classifier (gradient boosting + SHAP or attention-based deep learning) on this cohort's variant-to-phenotype associations and the deposited allele-frequency data (GVM000685), then embedding it in a human-AI collaboration loop where the model pre-ranks variants and flags uncertain cases for expert review, reducing turnaround time without sacrificing accuracy.

④ Integrating NIPT and WGS-NBS into a continuous prenatal-to-neonatal genomic pathway

Extending this paperThe QDnewborn study screens at birth (day 0–3), but a growing fraction of Chinese births now receive NIPT (non-invasive prenatal testing) for chromosomal aneuploidies during pregnancy. The corpus's large-scale NIPT study (10.1038/s44360-026-00104-2) covers Hebei province. A direct extension of the present paper: link NIPT outcomes from the prenatal record with WGS-NBS outcomes at birth for the same infant, measuring whether prenatal NIPT flags or misses the same monogenic conditions later confirmed by WGS, and modeling the diagnostic value of a combined prenatal-plus-neonatal pathway. This would require data linkage — a FAIR/open-science challenge the paper's current restricted-access raw-data policy must address.

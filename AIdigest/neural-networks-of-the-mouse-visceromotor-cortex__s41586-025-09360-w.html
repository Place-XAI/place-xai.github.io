Neural networks of the mouse visceromotor cortex

Hintiryan, Houri · Zhu, Muye · Zhao, Pingping · Zhang, Mingmin · Barry, Joshua · Nanda, Sumit · Rudd, Mitchell · Wong, Angela · Miller, Samara · Gou, Lin · Wei, Jinxing · Zingg, Brian · Sun, Jiandong · Gutierrez, Adriana · Mun, Hyun-Seung · Han, Yeji E. · Bowman, Ian · Garcia, Luis · Lo, Darrick · Boesen, Tyler · Cao, Chunru · Zhao, Qiuying · Foster, Nicholas N. · Moradi, Keivan · Yamashita, Seita · Estrada, Christian · Dev, Aishwarya · Gonzalez, Jennifer · Xu, Hanpeng · Yang, Gavin · Park, Chang Sin · Yang, X. Core Conclusion

Using integrated neuroanatomical, physiological and behavioural approaches in mice, this study constructs a comprehensive wiring diagram of the medial prefrontal cortex (MPF), revealing that the dorsal peduncular area (DP) — previously poorly understood — comprises deep (DPd) and superficial (DPs) sublayers that form the core of the visceromotor cortex. The DP functions as a network hub that integrates convergent cortical inputs and relays information in a predominantly unidirectional flow to regulate neuroendocrine, sympathetic and parasympathetic outputs. Notably, DPd neurons directly innervate hypothalamic CRH neurons to modulate stress-hormone (corticosterone) levels, establishing a novel direct cortical-to-neuroendocrine circuit. A unified MPF network model is proposed in which distinct MPF areas orchestrate physiological and behavioural responses to internal and external stimuli.

★

Why It Matters

  • Resolves a long-standing ambiguity in brain architecture. The DP had an inconsistent definition across mouse brain atlases; this paper definitively delineates it by cytoarchitecture, connectivity and gene expression, inserting a missing node into the MPF circuit map.
  • Discovers a direct cortical-to-neuroendocrine pathway. DPd neurons directly project to hypothalamic CRH neurons — bypassing the previously assumed indirect relays through the bed nucleus of the stria terminalis — and optogenetically activating these neurons acutely elevates plasma corticosterone, establishing a concrete mechanism linking prefrontal cognition to stress-hormone output.
  • Identifies the DP as a rare unidirectional cortical hub. Unlike virtually all other MPF structures (which are reciprocally interconnected), the DP receives convergent lateral-cortical input and relays it forward without significant feedback, a unique topological feature with broad implications for information routing in prefrontal networks.
  • Provides a platform resource for the field. The fully annotated MPF connectome cases and an interactive pathway-viewer (brain.neurobio.ucla.edu/mpf) together with open code (github.com/ucla-brain/mpf) make the data reusable by the wider neuroscience community.
  • Advances understanding of stress, autonomic and social-behaviour disorders. The DP is implicated in anxiety, depression, social defeat stress and fear conditioning — charting its circuits opens mechanistic targets for psychiatric interventions.

Core Methods

  • Neuroanatomical tract tracing: anterograde tracers (AAV, PHAL, BDA) and retrograde tracers (CTB, Fluorogold) injected into each MPF area (DPs, DPd, ILA, PL, ACAv, ACAd) and >96 upstream/downstream structures to map brain-wide input/output connectivity.
  • 2D hierarchical clustering: projection-fraction, input-fraction and reciprocity-fraction matrices for each MPF area constructed and clustered to reveal distinct network modules.
  • RNAscope in situ hybridization: cell-type-specific gene expression (>100 genes) validated DP laminar organization; combined with retrograde tracing to assign molecular identities to projection neuron types.
  • Sparse morphological labelling (MORF3): stochastic Cre-dependent reporter for 3D reconstruction of individual DP neuron dendritic arbours; imaged by light-sheet and confocal microscopy.
  • Trans-synaptic viral tracing: AAV1-Cre anterograde trans-synaptic tagging and TVA-mediated rabies retrograde tracing to map monosynaptic and polysynaptic circuits cell-type specifically.
  • Channelrhodopsin-assisted circuit mapping (CRACM): single-cell patch-clamp recordings combined with optogenetic stimulation of specific axon terminals to confirm monosynaptic functional connectivity (e.g., DPd → PVH CRH neurons, ENTl/PIR → DP).
  • Miniscope calcium imaging: GCaMP6f expressed in VGLUT2+ DP neurons; in-vivo recordings during social (same-sex, opposite-sex) and non-social (open-field, object exploration) behavioural trials; ROC analysis to identify significantly responsive cells.
  • Optogenetic behaviour: ChR2-mediated activation of specific DPd circuits (SUBv/BLAa → DPd → PVH) with plasma corticosterone assay to confirm functional neuroendocrine output.
  • Custom image analysis (Outspector): automated registration, thresholding and annotation pipeline for all tracer-label images, with 2D clustering for quality control across multiple injection replicates.

① Apply connectome-constrained network modelling to predict functional DP dynamics from the wiring diagram

Extending this paperThis paper produces a mesoscale structural connectome of the MPF but does not simulate or predict the dynamics that emerge from the wiring. A direct extension: use the paper's connectivity matrices as hard constraints in a spiking or rate-coded network model — as done for the fly visual system — to predict how activity flows through the DP hub under varying sensory or stress inputs. This would test whether the observed unidirectional topology is sufficient to explain the paper's calcium-imaging results (Fig. 5) and generate falsifiable predictions about prefrontal population codes.

Added value to my researchThe connectome-constrained modelling framework is analogous to physics-informed graph learning in spatial analysis: structural constraints (wiring/geography) are encoded as hard priors, and the network learns dynamics/flows on top. For my GeoAI work, this suggests encoding known spatial constraints (road networks, administrative boundaries) as fixed graph edges in a GNN and learning mobility or accessibility flows as emergent dynamics — a direct methodological transfer from neuroscience to urban analytics.

② Use whole-brain network statistics to extract topological signatures of the visceromotor hub

Extending this paperThe paper identifies the DP as a hub through qualitative observation of its projection patterns, but never quantifies this claim using formal network metrics (degree centrality, betweenness centrality, rich-club coefficient, modularity). Extending it: apply the full graph-theory toolkit used for the Drosophila whole-brain connectome to the paper's MPF connectivity matrices, deriving a quantitative "hubness score" and placing the DP within the broader mouse cortical network hierarchy. This would make the "network hub" label rigorously testable and comparable across species.

Added value to my researchNetwork-centrality metrics applied to brain connectomes translate directly to network community detection in urban systems — one of my core methods. The same metrics (betweenness centrality, modularity) that identify a cortical hub can identify spatial service hubs in healthcare or transport networks, informing more principled hub-and-spoke facility-location optimization.

③ Build a foundation model of DP neural activity that generalizes across stimuli and behavioural states

Extending this paperThe calcium-imaging experiments (Fig. 5) sample DP activity in a limited set of behavioural conditions (open field, same-sex interaction, opposite-sex interaction, object exploration). A direct extension: collect DP population recordings across a much wider battery of stimuli (stressors, appetitive cues, threat, social hierarchy contexts) and train a neural foundation model — using the paper's circuit-level architecture as an inductive bias — that predicts DP responses to new, unseen stimulus types. The paper's wiring diagram would constrain which input channels the model attends to.

Added value to my researchFoundation models trained on multi-context data with structural inductive biases are the exact architecture I pursue for geospatial foundation models: pre-training on diverse spatial contexts (POIs, mobility, remote sensing) with geographic structure as inductive bias, then fine-tuning for specific tasks (accessibility prediction, health outcomes). The neuroscience analogy clarifies why the structural prior (the connectome / the spatial graph) is the key ingredient that enables generalization.

④ Derive compact, interpretable computational models of the visceromotor cortex analogous to visual cortex models

Extending this paperThe paper establishes which areas are in the visceromotor cortex and who connects to whom, but not what computations the circuit performs. Extending it: build compact deep neural network models of DP responses (analogous to those built for visual cortex), where the model architecture mirrors the paper's established connectivity — input from olfactory, auditory, entorhinal and limbic streams; output to hypothalamus and brainstem. Fitting such a model to the paper's electrophysiology and calcium-imaging data would make the DP's information-integration function interpretable and quantitatively predictive.

⑤ Translate the DP's unidirectional hub topology into a design principle for spatial information-routing networks

Extending this paperThe paper's most surprising topological finding is that the DP breaks the MPF's default of reciprocal connectivity: it receives converging inputs from lateral cortical networks and routes them forward to medial and motor cortices with minimal feedback. A direct extension: investigate whether this asymmetric, hub-and-relay architecture is evolutionarily convergent across species by comparing DP-equivalent areas in primates using the paper's same tracing framework, and test whether the unidirectionality is functionally necessary by silencing reciprocal pathways with chemogenetics.

Added value to my researchThe DP's unidirectional topology — integrating diverse inputs and forwarding without feedback — is a compelling design principle for multi-source data fusion architectures in GeoAI. In my work fusing mobile phone, social media and remote-sensing streams, the analogy suggests treating one modality as the "DP layer" (convergence point) rather than using symmetric cross-attention across all modalities, potentially improving computational efficiency and interpretability of the fusion mechanism.

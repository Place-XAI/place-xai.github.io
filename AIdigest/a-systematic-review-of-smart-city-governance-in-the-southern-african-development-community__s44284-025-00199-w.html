A systematic review of smart city governance in the Southern African Development Community

Federica Duca · Amy Mutua · Lucille Tetley-Brown · Geci Karuri-Sebina · Judy Backhouse Published 2025-03-03 DOI 10.1038/s44284-025-00199-w Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

A qualitative systematic review of academic papers, policy documents and reports on smart cities across the Southern African Development Community (SADC) finds the field is dominated by technology- and infrastructure-centric framings imported from the Global North, while the region's actual urban dynamics, governance arrangements and digital-citizenship realities remain under-examined. The authors organize the literature into five research themes and argue that a credible smart-city agenda for SADC must foreground local, governance-oriented lenses and confront the impacts of digitalization and datafication rather than transplanting universal blueprints.

Core Methods

  • Qualitative systematic review: a structured, transparent search-and-screen procedure applied to both academic and policy literature, distinguishing it from conventional reviews that exclude grey literature.
  • Qualitative evidence synthesis: interpretive coding of included documents to surface recurring concepts, contradictions and silences (method grounded in Flemming et al., 2019).
  • Inductive thematic analysis: bottom-up derivation of the five research themes from the corpus rather than imposing a pre-set framework.
  • Knowledge–practice contrast: deliberately juxtaposing smart-city knowledge (academia) against smart-city practice (policy/reports) to expose the gap between them.
  • Critical/regional framing: reading the literature through a Global-South, governance-oriented lens to test the transferability of Northern smart-city paradigms to SADC.

Linear symmetric self-selecting 14-bit kinetic molecular memristors

Deepak Sharma · Santi Prasad Rath · Bidyabhusan Kundu · Anil Korkmaz · Harivignesh S · Damien Thompson · Navakanta Bhat · Sreebrata Goswami · R. Stanley Williams · Sreetosh Goswami Published 2024-09-11 DOI 10.1038/s41586-024-07902-2 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

A Ru-complex azo-aromatic molecular memristor achieves 14-bit (16,520-level) analog resolution with linear and symmetric conductance updates in a single time step — vastly beyond existing neuromorphic devices. Integrated into a self-selecting 64 × 64 crossbar, it performs vector–matrix multiplication (including Fourier transforms) in one step, achieving >73 dB signal-to-noise ratio (four orders of magnitude over state-of-the-art) while consuming 460× less energy than digital computers — a breakthrough that could extend neuromorphic computing from niche inference into the core of AI workloads.

Why It Matters

  • Breaks the resolution barrier. Existing neuromorphic circuit elements are limited to low-accuracy (typically 4–8 bit) operations. The 14-bit (16,520-level) molecular memristor unlocks signal processing, neural network training, and natural language processing — workloads currently reserved for power-hungry digital silicon.
  • Eliminates the selector problem. The device's intrinsic self-selection (unidirectional current) allows a selector-less crossbar array, removing a major integration hurdle that has blocked large-scale analog computing arrays.
  • Radical energy savings. The 460× energy reduction over digital computers could enable AI computation to move from cloud data centres to edge devices, democratizing AI access.
  • Precise kinetic control as a design principle. By engineering thermodynamic stability and nucleation kinetics of molecular transitions, the work shows that molecular electronic switching can be a precision instrument — not just a bistable switch — opening entirely new design space for organic electronics.

Core Methods

  • Kinetic molecular switching: precise voltage-pulse control over a nucleation-mediated transition between two thermodynamically stable electronic states in the Ru-azo-aromatic thin film, enabling fine-grained analog conductance programming.
  • Linear symmetric weight update: potentiation and depression are engineered to be equal-step and sign-symmetric, resolving the nonlinearity problem that plagues existing phase-change and oxide memristors.
  • Self-selecting crossbar architecture: device unidirectionality (rectifying I–V) inherently suppresses sneak-path currents, enabling a selector-free 64 × 64 crossbar — hardware vector–matrix multiplication in one time step.
  • Nucleation-based switching model: three-dimensional nucleation model (xy-plane, along film thickness z) fitted to experimental pulse-count data to explain and predict the 16,520-level analog response.
  • Spike-timing-dependent plasticity (STDP): Hebbian and anti-Hebbian learning rules implemented using two-spike waveform overlap, demonstrating biological synapse emulation.
  • VMM benchmarking: dot-product engine verified against known Fourier transform ground truth; SNR and energy metrics compared to state-of-the-art digital and analog baselines.

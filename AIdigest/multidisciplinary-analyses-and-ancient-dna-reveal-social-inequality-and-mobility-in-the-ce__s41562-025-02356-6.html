Multidisciplinary analyses and ancient DNA reveal social inequality and mobility in the Central Plains during the Eastern Zhou period in China

Baoshuai Zhang · Jiajing Zheng · Lei Sun · Yuanyuan Zhang · Zhenlong Gao · Zhaohui Han · Pengfei Sheng · … · Chuan-Chao Wang · Shaoqing Wen · Anchuan Fan Published 2025-12-30 DOI 10.1038/s41562-025-02356-6 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

By fusing proteomic sex identification, ancient DNA, and a stack of isotopes (carbon, nitrogen, strontium, oxygen) on 32 individuals from Songzhuang Cemetery, the study reconstructs a multidimensional portrait of social inequality in Eastern Zhou China (771–221 BC). Young women were systematically marginalized as sacrificial victims (22 of 26); diet stratified sharply by class and was set in childhood; nobles were disproportionately non-local migrants; and a genetic genealogy ties four noblewomen to a sacrificial victim, showing kinship and marital alliances upheld elite status — yet two individuals (tomb M18) show rare class mobility via childhood dietary shifts, proving the hierarchy was not perfectly rigid.

Why It Matters

  • Quantifies inequality across multiple axes at once. Instead of reading status off grave goods alone, the paper triangulates sex, diet, mobility, kinship, and class from the same skeletons — turning a historical narrative of hierarchy into measured, individual-level evidence.
  • Documents gendered violence with hard biomolecular data. Proteomic and aDNA sexing resolves osteological ambiguity and shows an overwhelming female bias among human sacrifices (85%), exposing the structural marginalization of women in early Chinese society.
  • Shows inequality is inherited from childhood. Serial dentin and enamel isotopes reveal that dietary class gaps were already locked in during early life — a deep-time analogue of how disadvantage compounds across the life course.
  • Captures rare upward and downward mobility. The M18 / M18X pair, whose isotopic life-histories cross at age ~10, gives a vivid bioarchaeological case of status reversal during the turbulent Eastern Zhou — evidence that even rigid systems leak.
  • Links kinship networks to the persistence of status. A century-spanning noble lineage and inter-regional marriage signals (southern gene flow into M18) show how families and alliances reproduced inequality across generations.

Core Methods

  • Proteomic sexing: AMELX/AMELY amelogenin peptide identification by nano-LC–MS/MS (MaxQuant), validated against aDNA-based X/Y coverage sexing and published osteology.
  • Palaeogenomics pipeline: AdapterRemoval → BWA aln (hs37d5) → DeDup → BamUtil trimming → SAMtools mpileup → pileupCaller pseudo-diploid genotypes; authenticity via PMDtools deamination, contamMix (mtDNA) and ANGSD (X-chromosome) contamination checks.
  • Kinship inference: pairwise mismatch (pMMRCalculator) relatedness coefficients plus READ2, requiring ≥10,000 overlapping SNPs — identifies four third-degree pairs forming one lineage.
  • Population genetics: smartPCA projection, unsupervised ADMIXTURE (K=2–9), outgroup f3, f4 statistics, and qpWave/qpAdm modelling (ADMIXTOOLS) — separates a southern-admixed "Songzhuang2" subgroup (~18.1% Tanshishan-related ancestry).
  • Multi-isotope geochemistry: 87Sr/86Sr (MC-ICP-MS) and δ18O for mobility/provenance; δ13C enamel + serial-dentin δ13C/δ15N for childhood diet trajectories; local bioavailable Sr range from fauna/flora baselines.
  • Statistics: Shapiro–Wilk normality, Student's t-test / Mann–Whitney U / Kruskal–Wallis H with Bonferroni correction (SPSS, Origin 2020); two-tailed, α = 0.05.

① Turn isotope provenance into a probabilistic geo-attribution model

Extending this paperThe paper localizes non-locals by checking whether 87Sr/86Sr and δ18O fall outside a mean ± 2 s.d. band, then reasons qualitatively toward the Qinling or Shandong coast. A direct extension: build a spatial isoscape (kriged bioavailable Sr/O surface) and run Bayesian geographic assignment so each individual gets a posterior probability map of childhood origin, with explicit uncertainty rather than a binary local/non-local call — sharpening the migration story the paper already tells.

Added value to my researchThis isoscape-plus-Bayesian-assignment pattern maps directly onto my multi-source data fusion and spatial-prediction work: an interpolated environmental surface feeding a probabilistic origin model is the same machinery I use to fuse remote sensing, POIs and mobile-phone signals into accessibility/exposure estimates with calibrated uncertainty.

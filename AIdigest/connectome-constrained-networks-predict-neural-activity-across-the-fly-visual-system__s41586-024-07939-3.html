Connectome-constrained networks predict neural activity across the fly visual system

Janne K. Lappalainen · Fabian D. Tschopp · Sridhama Prakhya · Mason McGill · Aljoscha Nern · Kazunori Shinomiya · Shin-ya Takemura · Eyal Gruntman · Jakob H. Macke · Srinivas C. Turaga Published 2024-09-11 DOI 10.1038/s41586-024-07939-3 Source nature.com ↗
Core Conclusion

Connectivity measurements alone — without any direct neural activity recordings — can predict the neural activity underlying visual motion computation in the fruit fly optic lobe. A connectome-constrained, task-optimized deep mechanistic network (DMN) of 64 cell types (45,669 neurons, 1,513,231 synapses) trained only to estimate optic flow correctly predicts ON/OFF contrast selectivity and direction selectivity for cell types validated across 26 independent experimental studies. Crucially, this strategy succeeds because biological neural networks are sparsely connected — a universal property that constrains the solution space enough for connectivity alone to determine function.

Why It Matters

  • Settles a foundational debate in systems neuroscience. It was unclear whether connectome measurements alone — without direct physiology — could constrain neural computation. This paper demonstrates they can, at single-neuron resolution across 64 identified cell types.
  • Turns the connectome into a predictive engine. Rather than describing wiring diagrams statically, the DMN framework generates detailed, experimentally testable hypotheses for every neuron's response tuning — validated across 26 prior studies.
  • Identifies sparsity as the key enabler. Sparse connectivity (a universal feature of biological neural networks across species and brain regions) dramatically reduces the solution space, making task-optimization converge on biologically realistic mechanisms rather than arbitrary optima.
  • Provides a reusable open platform. All trained models, data, and interactive notebooks are released at github.com/TuragaLab/flyvis, offering the community a working simulation of the fly visual system that generates predictions for untested stimuli and cell types.
  • Bridges AI and neuroscience at unprecedented detail. Unlike prior task-optimized models of mammalian visual pathways that align only at the population/layer level, every neuron and synapse in the DMN maps to a known biological counterpart.

64 cell types, 45,669 neurons, 1,513,231 synapses, arranged in a hexagonal lattice of 721 columns modelling the central visual field of the right eye (~700–900 ommatidia). Free parameters reduced to 734 (from >2 billion in an unconstrained equivalent).
Training dataset

Validation studies

Synthetic benchmark
MNIST handwritten digit dataset: used to test the sparsity hypothesis with synthetic feedforward networks of varying connectivity density (25 ground-truth networks per sparsity level, 6 hidden layers, 128 neurons/layer).

Cell-type-specific transcriptomics (ref. 30 in the paper) — used to assign synaptic sign (excitatory/inhibitory) per cell-type pair from neurotransmitter and receptor expression profiling.

Core Methods

  • Connectome assembly: FIB-25 and FIB-19 FIB-SEM reconstructions merged via expectation-maximization algorithm aligning synaptic statistics and hand-annotated column references; spatial translation invariance assumed (hexagonal convolutional tiling) to build a consensus connectome of 64 cell types across 721 columns.
  • Deep mechanistic network (DMN): recurrent neural network with threshold-linear (leaky integrator) neuronal dynamics; synaptic weights proportional to connectome synapse counts scaled by a trainable per-type unitary strength; signs fixed from transcriptomics. Implemented as a hexagonal convolutional RNN in PyTorch with Euler integration (Δt = 20 ms).
  • Task optimization via backpropagation through time: 734 biophysical parameters (neuron time constants, resting potentials, unitary synapse scales) optimized jointly with a 2-layer convolutional decoder to minimize L2 optic-flow prediction error on Sintel naturalistic video sequences.
  • Ensemble approach: 50 independently initialized models trained; ensemble reflects diversity of parameter configurations consistent with connectome + task constraints; top-10 by task performance used for primary analyses.
  • UMAP + Gaussian mixture clustering: nonlinear dimensionality reduction of high-dimensional naturalistic-stimulus response vectors per cell type across ensemble; clusters reveal qualitatively distinct mechanistic hypotheses (e.g., 3 T4c clusters corresponding to different tuning mechanisms).
  • Ablation studies: 7 DMN variants with systematically varying connectome constraints (connectivity only / with synapse counts / with signs / fully optimized) to isolate which data are necessary for accurate predictions.
  • Sparsity simulation: feedforward synthetic networks on MNIST with lottery-ticket pruning; Pearson correlation of neural responses between ground-truth and simulated connectome networks as a function of connectivity density.

① Scale the DMN framework to a whole-brain connectome using the new Drosophila atlas

Extending this paperThis paper models 64 cell types from partial optic-lobe FIB-SEM reconstructions assembled semi-manually. The contemporaneous whole-brain Drosophila connectome (referenced in the Discussion) now provides network-level statistics for the entire brain. A direct extension is to apply the DMN strategy — connectome constraints + task optimization — to this complete atlas, thereby testing whether the sparsity-accuracy relationship holds at whole-brain scale, and whether sensorimotor integration tasks can be predicted from connectivity alone. The challenge would be specifying behaviorally meaningful tasks beyond optic flow for multi-region circuits.

Added value to my researchThe same strategy of constraining a large network model with structural measurements and optimizing for task performance maps onto my physics-informed graph learning agenda: structural constraints (road networks, administrative boundaries, population density grids) act as the "connectome," and spatiotemporal prediction objectives (disease spread, accessibility) play the role of the task. Formalizing this analogy would let me import DMN ensemble training and UMAP-based hypothesis clustering into GeoAI spatial prediction models.

② Replace the fixed-sign neurotransmitter assignment with a learned sign inference layer

Extending this paperThe DMN fixes synaptic signs from transcriptomics; for a minority of cell types, signs are guessed. A direct extension: treat sign as a latent binary variable jointly inferred during task optimization — essentially an expectation-maximization loop that alternates between sign assignment and biophysical parameter estimation. This would reduce dependence on incomplete transcriptomic data and could reveal ambiguous cell types whose sign is computationally determined, not anatomically settled. The compact deep neural network models of the visual cortex (corpus) show that architectural choices in model initialization strongly shape what is learned.

Added value to my researchThis connects to my explainable machine learning for spatial prediction: in spatial regression models (GWR/MGWR), the sign of spatial autocorrelation (positive vs. negative spillover) is often assumed rather than inferred. Treating sign as a latent variable optimized jointly with model parameters would yield sign-aware spatially varying coefficient models with principled uncertainty.

Cross-ref (corpus): Compact deep neural network models of the visual cortex — 10.1038/s41586-026-10150-1

③ Pre-train the DMN on unsupervised statistics before task-specific fine-tuning

Extending this paperThe paper trains 50 DMNs from scratch on a single optic-flow task. A powerful extension: first pre-train the DMN on unsupervised statistics of naturalistic visual input (e.g., sparse coding, contrast normalization) before task-specific fine-tuning. The corpus paper on unsupervised pretraining in biological neural networks shows this matches the developmental sequence of biological visual systems and produces representations more aligned with neural recordings. For the DMN, unsupervised pretraining could reduce ensemble variance, improve transfer to novel stimuli, and reveal whether the 50-model diversity collapses to fewer mechanistic clusters.

Added value to my researchThis directly informs my work on fine-tuning geospatial foundation models: pre-training on task-agnostic spatial statistics (e.g., urban morphology, land-use co-occurrence) before fine-tuning on health or accessibility outcomes is the exact analogue. The DMN pre-training results would provide principled support for why pre-training reduces the number of fine-tuning samples needed.

Cross-ref (corpus): Unsupervised pretraining in biological neural networks — 10.1038/s41586-025-09180-y

④ Build a foundation model of Drosophila neural activity from DMN ensemble outputs

Extending this paperThe paper generates 50 DMN models, each making predictions for 64 cell types across hundreds of stimuli. The corpus paper on neural activity foundation models shows that large-scale pre-training on diverse stimulus-response data enables generalization to unseen stimulus types. Treating the DMN ensemble's simulated activity as training data — augmenting sparse real recordings — would allow training a foundation model that generates predictions for novel visual contexts, missing cell types, and unstimulated conditions. This is a natural follow-on that the FlyVis codebase already partially enables.

⑤ Use the DMN's critical initialization insights to bootstrap spatial network models

Extending this paperThe paper notes (Discussion) that the ensemble reflects diverse local optima, and that task error correlates with tuning accuracy (r = 0.60, p = 2.6 × 10⁻⁶). This implies that initialization matters: models starting far from a biologically realistic configuration take longer to converge or miss the correct mechanism. A direct extension: use the paper's own ensemble to identify critical initialization points — connectome-informed parameter seeds — and test whether biologically constrained initialization shrinks the ensemble variance in follow-on experiments with richer (mammalian) connectomes. The corpus paper on critical initialization for biological neural networks provides exactly this theoretical foundation.

Added value to my researchIn my spatiotemporal kernel density estimation and graph learning models, initialization of graph neural network weights from spatial structure (adjacency derived from road networks or service catchment areas) is analogous. The DMN demonstration that structural initialization reduces free parameters from >2 billion to 734 motivates using spatial topology as a strong prior in my GNN-based accessibility and mobility models — drastically improving sample efficiency for training with limited ground-truth observations.
